Common foods with carbohydrates include:
  • Grains: Bread, pasta, rice, oats, barley, quinoa, couscous, bulgur, etc.
  • Cereals: Breakfast cereals, including oatmeal, cornflakes, bran flakes, etc.
  • Starchy Vegetables: Potatoes, sweet potatoes, corn, peas, squash, pumpkin, etc.
  • Legumes: Beans (black beans, kidney beans, pinto beans, etc.), lentils, chickpeas, etc.
  • Fruits: Apples, bananas, oranges, berries, grapes, mangoes, pineapples, etc.
  • Dairy: Milk, yogurt, kefir, and dairy-based desserts like ice cream and pudding (contain lactose, a natural sugar).
  • Sugary Snacks and Sweets: Cookies, cakes, pastries, candies, chocolates, etc.
  • Sugary Beverages: Regular sodas, fruit juices, fruit drinks, energy drinks, sports drinks, sweetened teas, etc.
  • Sugary Condiments and Spreads: Jam, jelly, honey, maple syrup, chocolate spreads, etc.
  • Processed Foods: Many processed foods contain added sugars and refined carbohydrates, including certain breakfast bars, snacks, and packaged desserts.
Common foods with protein include:
  • Meat: Beef, chicken, pork, lamb, turkey, etc.
  • Seafood: Fish (salmon, tuna, trout, etc.), shrimp, scallops, crab, etc.
  • Eggs: Chicken eggs are a common source of protein.
  • Dairy: Cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, feta, etc.), cottage cheese, Greek yogurt, etc.
  • Legumes: Beans (black beans, kidney beans, chickpeas, etc.), lentils, peas, etc.
  • Nuts and seeds: Almonds, peanuts, walnuts, sunflower seeds, chia seeds, etc.
  • Soy products: Tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk, etc.
Common foods with fats include:
  • Oils: Olive oil, coconut oil, avocado oil, canola oil, etc.
  • Butter and ghee: Used in cooking and baking.
  • Avocado: A source of healthy fats.
  • Nuts and seeds: Almonds, cashews, peanuts, sesame seeds, etc.
  • Fatty fish: Salmon, mackerel, sardines, trout, etc.
  • Nut butters: Peanut butter, almond butter, etc.
  • Cheese: Some cheeses are higher in fat content, such as cheddar, brie, and gouda.
  • Whole milk: Contains fat unless it's skim or low-fat milk.
  • Dark chocolate: Contains cocoa butter, which is a source of healthy fats.
